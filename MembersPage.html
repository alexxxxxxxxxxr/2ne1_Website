THE STORY OF 2NE1

"From Trainees to Trailblazers: The Journey That Changed K-pop Forever"

2004-2008

The Formation Years

The story began when Yang Hyun-suk, founder of YG Entertainment, envisioned a girl group that would break every convention in K-pop. Unlike the typical "cute and innocent" concept dominating the industry, he wanted something bold, fierce, and unprecedented.

The members were carefully selected:

  • Park Bom auditioned three times for YG and was initially set to debut as a solo artist in 2007.
  • Sandara Park returned from the Philippines where she was already a celebrity, joining YG in 2007.
  • CL (Lee Chae-rin) trained for 4 years and was chosen as the leader for her charisma and rap skills.
  • Minzy (Gong Minji) was discovered through an audition video at age 13, becoming the youngest member.
2009

The Name: 2NE1

The name "2NE1" holds multiple meanings, reflecting the group's innovative spirit:

  • "New Evolution of the 21st Century" - Representing their mission to revolutionize K-pop
  • "To Anyone" - The Korean pronunciation, symbolizing music for everyone
  • "Twenty-One" - The English pronunciation, representing youth and prime years
  • 2 (NE) 1 - "Two Energies United as One" - Four distinct individuals creating one powerful force

The unconventional spelling with "NE" instead of "N" made them instantly recognizable and unforgettable.

ESTABLISHED 2009

BlackJacks: The Unbreakable Bond

On August 7, 2009, 2NE1's official fandom was named "BlackJacks" - inspired by the strongest hand in the card game Blackjack (an ace and a card worth 10 points).

The symbolism was powerful:

  • 2NE1 = The Ace (best card in the deck)
  • Fans = The Jack (worth 10 points)
  • Together = Blackjack, the unbeatable combination

The official fandom color is Hot Pink, representing the group's bold, passionate, and vibrant energy.

BlackJacks remain one of K-pop's most dedicated fandoms, keeping 2NE1's legacy alive even during their hiatus.

2016 - PRESENT

The End, and The Return

On November 25, 2016, YG Entertainment officially announced 2NE1's disbandment, ending an era. CL released the farewell song "Goodbye," written in just 10 minutes as an emotional final message to fans.

But the story didn't end there...

In April 2022, 2NE1 reunited for a surprise performance at Coachella, becoming the first K-pop girl group to perform at the festival. The emotional reunion showed that their bond—and their impact—was eternal.

In 2024, the group officially reunited as a complete four-member group for their "Welcome Back" tour, proving that legends never truly fade away.

"We were apart, but we were never broken. 2NE1 is forever." - CL
